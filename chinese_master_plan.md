# Chinese Master Plan 🇨🇳

This plan follows the HSK 3.0 standard to guide you from zero to basic conversational Mandarin over 12 weeks.

---

## 📚 Master Topic List
Mandarin is tonal and character-based. Here is your focus:

### 1. The Foundation (Pinyin & Tones)
Mandarin is a tonal language. The same sound with a different tone has a completely different meaning.

| Tone | Pinyin | Description | Analogy | Example (ma) | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st Tone** | **ā** | High and level | Like a robot or a long "ahhh" | **mā** | Mother (妈) |
| **2nd Tone** | **á** | Rising | Like asking a question ("What?") | **má** | Hemp (麻) |
| **3rd Tone** | **ǎ** | Falling-Rising | A low, dipping sound | **mǎ** | Horse (马) |
| **4th Tone** | **à** | Falling | Sharp, short, like a command | **mà** | Scold (骂) |
| **Neutral** | **a** | Short & light | Quick and soft | **ma** | (Question part.) |

- **Tone Sandhi**: Rules for when tones change (e.g., when two 3rd tones meet, the first becomes a 2nd tone).

### 2. Characters (Hanzi)
- **Radicals**: The 214 building blocks of all characters.
- **Stroke Order**: The correct way to write characters.
- **HSK 1-3 Vocabulary**: High-frequency words for daily life.

### 3. Grammar Structures
- **SVO Structure**: Similar to English (Subject-Verb-Object).
- **Measure Words (Classifiers)**: Using words like "Ge" (个) correctly.
- **Particles**: Le (Completed), Ma (Question), Ba (Suggestion).
- **Negation**: Bu (不) vs. Mei (没).

## 🛠️ Weekly Study Method (The 4-Step Cycle)
Use this cycle every week to ensure you don't just "read" the rules, but actually "use" them.

1.  **Day 1-2: Learn (Input)**
    *   Watch a video or read a guide on the weekly topic.
    *   Find 5 real-world examples in a book or news article.
2.  **Day 3-4: Practice (Controlled)**
    *   Complete exercises or fill-in-the-blank tasks.
3.  **Day 5-6: Create (Output)**
    *   Write a 50-word paragraph using that week's grammar.
4.  **Day 7: Review**
    *   Check for errors. (You can ask me to "Audit" your writing).
