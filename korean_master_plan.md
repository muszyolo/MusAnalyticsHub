# Korean Master Plan 🇰🇷

This plan is designed to take you from a complete beginner to a confident user of basic Korean over 12 weeks.

---

## 📚 Master Topic List
Korean is an SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) language. Here is what you need to master:

### 1. The Script & Sound (Hangul)
- **Vowels & Consonants**: Learning the 24 basic letters.
- **Batchim**: Final consonants that change pronunciation.
- **Syllable Blocks**: How to read and write in blocks, not lines.

### 2. Grammar Pillars (Particles)
- **Topic Particles (은/는)**: Marking what you are talking about.
- **Subject Particles (이/가)**: Marking who is doing the action.
- **Object Particles (을/를)**: Marking what is receiving the action.
- **Time/Place Particles (에/에서)**.

### 3. Verb Conjugation & Speech Levels
- **Polite Form (-아요/-어요)**: Used in most daily situations.
- **Formal Form (-습니다)**: Used in business or news.
- **Informal/Casual (Panmal)**: Used with close friends.
- **Tenses**: Past, Present, and Future endings.

## 📅 The 12-Week Roadmap
Follow this schedule to move through the basics systematically.

| Phase | Week | Topic Focus | Key Concepts |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1: Hangul** | **1** | Basic Vowels & Consonants | Reading and writing your name. |
| | **2** | Batchim & Double Consonants | Complex pronunciation rules. |
| **Phase 2: Basics** | **3** | To be (이다 / 있다) | Identifying things and locations. |
| | **4** | Basic Action Verbs | Present tense conjugation (-아요/어요). |
| | **5** | Topic & Subject Particles | Mastering 은/는 vs 이/가. |
| **Phase 3: Building** | **6** | Past Tense | Describing yesterday's events. |
| | **7** | Numbers (Sino & Native) | Counting things vs. telling time. |
| | **8** | Object Particles & Negation | "I don't eat meat" (안/못). |
| **Phase 4: Fluency** | **9** | Connecting Sentences | Using "and" (-고) and "but" (-지만). |
| | **10** | Future Tense & Intent | Making plans for the weekend. |
| | **11** | Honorifics (Si-da) | Speaking respectfully to elders. |
| | **12** | Adjectives & Modifiers | "The pretty flower" vs "The flower is pretty." |

## 🛠️ Weekly Study Method (The 4-Step Cycle)
Use this cycle every week to ensure you don't just "read" the rules, but actually "use" them.

1.  **Day 1-2: Learn (Input)**
    *   Watch a video or read a guide on the weekly topic.
    *   Find 5 real-world examples in a book or news article.
2.  **Day 3-4: Practice (Controlled)**
    *   Complete exercises or fill-in-the-blank tasks.
3.  **Day 5-6: Create (Output)**
    *   Write a 50-word paragraph using that week's grammar.
4.  **Day 7: Review**
    *   Check for errors. (You can ask me to "Audit" your writing).
