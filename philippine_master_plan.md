# Philippine Master Plan 🇵🇭

This plan is designed to take you from a complete beginner to a confident user of conversational Filipino (Tagalog) over 12 weeks.

---

## 📚 Master Topic List
Tagalog is known for its unique verb system and flexible word order. Here is what you need to master:

### 1. The Foundation (Pronunciation)
- **Abakada**: The traditional Tagalog alphabet and modern modifications.
- **Phonetics**: Tagalog is spoken exactly as written. Focus on the "Ng" sound and glottal stops.
- **Taglish**: Understanding how English words are integrated into daily speech.

### 2. Grammar Pillars (Pronouns & Particles)
- **Pronouns (Ang, Ng, Sa)**: The three cases of pronouns (Subjective, Objective, Directional).
- **Enclitics**: Small words that change the feeling of a sentence (Na, Pa, Man, Ba, Nga).
- **Politeness**: Using "Po" and "Opo" to show respect.

### 3. Verb Focus System (The Core)
- **Actor Focus (Mag-, -um-)**: When the person doing the action is the focus.
- **Object Focus (-in, -an, i-)**: When the thing being acted upon is the focus.
- **Tenses**: Contemplated (Future), Imperfective (Present), and Perfective (Past).

## 📅 The 12-Week Roadmap
Follow this schedule to move through the levels of Tagalog.

| Phase | Week | Topic Focus | Key Concepts |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1: Sounds** | **1** | Alphabet & Greetings | Kumusta, Salamat, and Abakada. |
| | **2** | Personal Introductions | "Ako si..." and basic self-info. |
| **Phase 2: Verbs** | **3** | Actor Focus Verbs (-um-) | Simple actions like "To eat" (Kumain). |
| | **4** | Present & Past Tenses | Talking about what you did today. |
| | **5** | Enclitics (Ba, Na, Pa) | Turning statements into questions. |
| **Phase 3: Building** | **6** | Object Focus Verbs (-in) | Focusing on the thing you are using. |
| | **7** | Pronouns (Ang vs. Ng) | "I" (Ako) vs "My" (Ko). |
| | **8** | Numbers & Adjectives | Ma- adjectives (Maganda, Mabuti). |
| **Phase 4: Fluency** | **9** | Directions & Locations | Sa particles and giving directions. |
| | **10** | Taglish & Slang | Natural daily communication. |
| | **11** | Complex Sentences | Using "because" (dahil) and "if" (kung). |
| | **12** | Full Conversation | Review and final polish. |

## 🛠️ Weekly Study Method (The 4-Step Cycle)
Use this cycle every week to ensure you are actually "using" the language.

1.  **Day 1-2: Learn (Input)**
    *   Watch a video or read a guide on the weekly topic.
    *   Find 5 real-world examples in a Tagalog song or drama.
2.  **Day 3-4: Practice (Controlled)**
    *   Complete exercises or fill-in-the-blank tasks.
3.  **Day 5-6: Create (Output)**
    *   Write a 50-word paragraph using that week's grammar.
4.  **Day 7: Review**
    *   Check for errors. (You can ask me to "Audit" your writing).
