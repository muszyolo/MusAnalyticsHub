## рџ“љ Master Topic List
Before following the schedule, here is the complete list of grammar pillars you will master:

### 1. Parts of Speech (The Basics)
- **Nouns**: Countable, Uncountable, Abstract, Proper/Common.
- **Pronouns**: Subject, Object, Possessive, Relative (Who, Which).
- **Adjectives & Adverbs**: Comparison (better, most), placement.
- **Prepositions**: Time (In/On/At), Place, and Direction.
- **Conjunctions**: Joining words (And, But, Because, Although).

### 2. Verb Tenses (The Timeline)
- **Present**: Simple, Continuous, Perfect.
- **Past**: Simple, Continuous, Perfect.
- **Future**: Will, Going to, Future Continuous.

### 3. Advanced Logic (The Polish)
- **Conditionals**: If-sentences (Zero, 1st, 2nd, 3rd).
- **Voice**: Active vs. Passive voice.
- **Speech**: Reported (Indirect) speech.
- **Verb Forms**: Gerunds (-ing) vs. Infinitives (to...).
- **Phrasal Verbs**: Natural expressions (e.g., *give up, look into*).

## рџ› пёЏ Weekly Study Method (The 4-Step Cycle)
Use this cycle every week to ensure you don't just "read" the rules, but actually "use" them.

1.  **Day 1-2: Learn (Input)**
    *   Watch a video or read a guide on the weekly topic.
    *   Find 5 real-world examples in a book or news article.
2.  **Day 3-4: Practice (Controlled)**
    *   Complete exercises or fill-in-the-blank tasks.
3.  **Day 5-6: Create (Output)**
    *   Write a 50-word paragraph using that week's grammar.
4.  **Day 7: Review**
    *   Check for errors. (You can ask me to "Audit" your writing).
